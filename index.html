A Dream She Keeps Returning To

A woman wakes inside a house with no clocks. The windows are bricked, the mirrors veiled. Each room feels like memory, but none of it is hers. Outside, voices call her name, but they don’t use the right tone. She moves from door to door, looking for the version of herself that can answer. It’s not a nightmare. It’s Tuesday.

Written in fragments. Built in silence.
